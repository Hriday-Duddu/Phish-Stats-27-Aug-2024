What is Coin base?

Coinbase Global Inc (Coinbase) operates as a cryptocurrency exchange platform. The company provides a platform for trading, staking, safekeeping, spending and transferring crypto assets. It also carries out blockchain-powered technologies including self-custody wallets, decentralized apps and open community engagement platforms. Coinbase trading platform includes Consumer Trading, Coinbase Prime and Coinbase Wallet. The company’s platform provides real-time market information, value-added services, market infrastructure, and volume-based pricing, and the Coinbase Wallet platform enables merchants and consumers to transact crypto coins. It operates in the UK, Switzerland, Canada, Ireland, Germany and the US. Coinbase is headquartered in Wilmington, Delaware, the US.

Coinbase Wallet is a self-custody wallet that gives you complete control of your crypto. This means that the private keys (that represent ownership of the cryptocurrency) for your Wallet are stored directly on your mobile device and not with a centralized exchange like Coinbase.com.

Who is Coinbase for?

Coinbase is a comprehensive cryptocurrency exchange. It lets you buy, sell, exchange, store and spend cryptocurrency. The platform is a good choice for anyone interested in investing in bitcoin, for example, whether you are a beginner or an active trader.

For beginners — Coinbase offers a small minimum initial investment of $2 in your local currency, as well as plenty of educational resources to help you learn the ropes.

For active traders — Coinbase offers a plan that has some of the lowest commissions in the industry. Plus you can trade over 150 cryptocurrencies, including all the most popular ones.

And as we'll see shortly, Coinbase even gives you an opportunity to earn more crypto on the balance that you already have in your account.

How to secure login?

Securing your login is crucial to protecting your online accounts, especially for platforms like Coinbase that deal with sensitive financial information. Here are some steps you can take to secure your login:

1. Use Strong, Unique Passwords

Create a Complex Password: Use a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.

Avoid Reusing Passwords: Never use the same password across multiple sites.

Use a Password Manager: Tools like LastPass or 1Password can generate and store complex passwords for you.

2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Set Up 2FA on Coinbase: Go to your Coinbase account settings and enable 2FA. You can use apps like Google Authenticator or Authy for generating the 2FA codes.

Use SMS as a Backup: Although less secure than an authenticator app, SMS 2FA is better than nothing.

3. Monitor Account Activity

Regularly Check Login History: Look for any unfamiliar devices or locations accessing your account.

Set Up Alerts: Enable notifications for any account changes or login attempts.

4. Keep Your Email Secure

Use a Secure Email Provider: Choose a reputable provider with strong security measures.

Enable 2FA on Your Email: Securing your email is vital since it’s often the recovery method for your other accounts.

5. Be Wary of Phishing Attacks

Verify URLs: Always check the URL before entering your login details. Ensure it’s the official Coinbase site.

Avoid Clicking on Links in Emails: Instead, manually type the website’s URL into your browser.

Use Anti-Phishing Tools: Consider browser extensions or security software that alerts you to potential phishing sites.

6. Keep Your Devices Secure

Update Software Regularly: Ensure your operating system, browser, and antivirus software are up to date.

Use Antivirus Software: Protect your devices from malware and keyloggers.

Avoid Public Wi-Fi: If you must use public Wi-Fi, use a VPN to encrypt your connection.

7. Secure Recovery Options

Update Security Questions: Choose security questions that are difficult to guess or find the answers to online.

Review Account Recovery Options: Ensure your recovery options (email, phone number) are up-to-date and secure.

8. Logout After Use

Always Log Out: Especially when using a shared or public computer.

Clear Browsing Data: If using a public computer, clear cookies and cache after logging out.
